Axiom Demo Note — Geopolitics Brief (Test Knowledge)

Last updated: May 2026

The South China Sea remains a major flashpoint. China claims most of the sea via the
"nine-dash line"; Vietnam, the Philippines, Malaysia, Brunei, and Taiwan dispute parts
of that claim. In 2016 the Permanent Court of Arbitration ruled against China's historic
claims under UNCLOS, but Beijing rejected the ruling.

NATO expanded significantly after Russia's 2022 full-scale invasion of Ukraine. Finland
joined NATO in April 2023 and Sweden joined in March 2024. Western allies have supplied
Ukraine with artillery, air defense, and later F-16 fighter jets under coordinated aid packages.

The Abraham Accords (2020) normalized relations between Israel and several Arab states
including the UAE and Bahrain. Morocco also joined the accords in exchange for US recognition
of its sovereignty over Western Sahara.

India is the world's most populous country as of 2023, surpassing China. It holds the
G20 presidency rotation and positions itself as a leader of the Global South while
maintaining strategic partnerships with the US, Russia, and France simultaneously.

The BRICS grouping expanded in 2024 to include Egypt, Ethiopia, Iran, and the UAE alongside
Brazil, Russia, India, China, and South Africa. The bloc discusses dedollarization and
alternative payment systems but remains economically diverse.

The Taiwan Strait is watched closely by US Indo-Pacific Command. US policy follows
"strategic ambiguity" — unofficial relations with Taiwan under the Taiwan Relations Act
without formal diplomatic recognition, while opposing unilateral status changes.

Iran's nuclear program is monitored by the IAEA. After the 2018 US withdrawal from the
JCPOA nuclear deal, Tehran gradually exceeded enrichment limits. Indirect US-Iran talks
on a renewed framework have stalled repeatedly since 2022.

Climate diplomacy: the Paris Agreement target is to limit warming to 1.5°C above pre-industrial
levels. COP28 (Dubai, 2023) produced the first call to "transition away from fossil fuels"
in a UN climate summit text, though language was contested by oil-exporting states.

Key capitals reference: Kyiv (Ukraine), Taipei (Taiwan), Tehran (Iran), New Delhi (India),
Brasília (Brazil — BRICS founding member).
